Where it all began

I grew up in a household where the kitchen was the centre of everything. My mother cooked without recipes, without measurements — a handful of this, a pinch of that, a glance at the pot and somehow everything was perfect. I spent years watching her, trying to absorb it all without knowing I was doing it.

It was only when I moved away from home that I understood what those hours in the kitchen had given me. Not just recipes, but a language — a way of expressing care, nostalgia, and belonging through food.

"The smell of mustard oil in a hot pan takes me home every single time."

Why I started this site

The truth is, I was worried. Worried that the recipes I grew up with — the ones that live in memory and muscle, not in any book — would one day be lost. My mother never wrote them down. Her mother never wrote them down. And so I decided to do it for them.

Every recipe on this site comes with the best of my memory and the honest admission that there is no single "correct" version. My Aloo Posto will be slightly different from yours, and both will be right. That is the nature of home cooking — it lives and breathes and changes with each pair of hands.

What you'll find here

Mostly Bengali food — the cuisine I know best and love most deeply. Fish cooked in mustard. Dal fragrant with ghee and coconut. Sweets that take patience. But also the everyday dishes: quick weeknight meals, tea-time snacks, things I make when I miss home and need comfort fast.

Each recipe is tested in my kitchen, written as clearly as I can manage, and accompanied by whatever notes I think will actually help. I hope you find something here that makes its way into your regular rotation — and into your memory too.
